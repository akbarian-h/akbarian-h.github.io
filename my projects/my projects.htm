Design and simulation of an isolated-multiple output SEPIC dc/dc converter based on a non-isolated single output SEPIC converter for automotive applications

 

Based on a non isolated single output SEPIC converter for the automotive application [1], an isolated multiple output converters with the same topology is designed and simulated in Psim. To investigate working property of the converter under different load conditions, a step response of the output with load variation is simulated. To have a better understanding of the harmonic injection of the converter, a FFT analysis in the output is done. In addition, some non-ideal parameters like Req and Leq of the non ideal transformer is discussed.

Because of their wide input/output voltage ranges, SEPIC converters are vastly used in the industry specifically in applications in which a battery or variable power supply (e.g. Automotive, MPPT[1]) is involved. The main advantage of the SEPIC converters is the output positive and the wide output range which can be less, equal or greater than the input. This characteristic makes them suitable for PV[2] converters and automotive applications.

Supervisory Control of an Ozone Generator System (OGS) with DES Theory

 

In the following report, a local controller is provided using NCT[1] to control and satisfy some specifications of an Ozone Generator System (OGS). Figure 1 shows physical model of the OGS.

Three steps in designing a supervisor control systems are followed: modeling the system(Plant), defining appropriate automata satisfying particular specification, and finally designing the supervisor, such that restrict the closed and marking behavior of the plant under an admissible premise with respect to the specifications and safety issues are presented.

A minimally restricted admissible (Controllable) and non-blocking supervisor has been designed such that achieve desired marking and closed behavior of the system; using DECK[2].

The aim of this report is to provide steps in designing a controller to control production of ozone to be used for water treatment.

Ozone is formed from deoxygenate by the action of ultraviolet light and also atmospheric electrical discharges, and is present in low concentrations throughout the Earth's atmosphere. [5]

Oxygen enters to the OGS tank through valve controlled pipes (V2, V3) and then passes through two different potential electrodes connected to a high voltage AC source (PSU); electrical discharge which produces ozone.

The resulting ozone will be then kept in contact with water to be treated.


[1] Non-blocking Supervisory Control Theory

[2] Discrete Event Control Kit (DES function software package written in MATLAB [6]).
